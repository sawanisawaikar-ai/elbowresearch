# Lateral Epicondyle Tendinopathy: Summary of Evidence

## Source
University of British Columbia (2016)

## Overview
Lateral epicondyle tendinopathy (LET), commonly known as tennis elbow, is a degenerative condition affecting the tendons on the outer elbow.

## Key Findings

### Definition
- Also called: tennis elbow, lateral epicondylitis
- Affects 1-3% of general population
- Degenerative process, not primarily inflammatory

### Risk Factors
- Repetitive forearm motions
- High-force activities
- Awkward postures
- Vibration exposure

### Clinical Research Evidence

| Treatment | Evidence | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Manual Therapy | No specific evidence in acute stage | Further research needed |
| Shock Wave Therapy (SWT) | Mixed results | May help chronic cases |
| Low Level Laser Therapy | Some positive | Consider as adjunct |
| Eccentric Exercises | Strong evidence | Recommended |
| Counterforce Bracing | Moderate evidence | Recommended |

### Evidence Categories
- **Level I**: Systematic reviews, RCTs
- **Level II**: Individual RCTs
- **Level III**: Controlled trials without randomization
- **Level IV**: Case-control studies
- **Level V**: Expert opinion

### Treatment Recommendations

**Conservative approaches (first line):**
1. Eccentric strengthening exercises
2. Manual therapy
3. Counterforce bracing
4. Activity modification

**Outcomes:**
- Most patients respond well to conservative treatment
- Recovery typically takes 6-12 weeks
- Recurrence is common if activity not modified
