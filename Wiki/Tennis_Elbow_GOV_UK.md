# Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis) - UK Clinical Guidelines

## Source
GOV.UK - Health and Safety Executive

## Overview
Tennis elbow is one of the most common conditions to affect the arm, causing pain in the region of the lateral epicondyle of the elbow with associated impairment of grip that may restrict activities of daily living.

## Key Findings

### Causes
- **Mechanical overload** - primary cause during sports or work
- **Repetitive activity** - flexion/extension or pronation/supination of forearm
- **Overuse** of extensor musculature
- **Psychosocial factors** may also play a role

### Most Affected Groups
- Mechanics
- Builders
- Domestic workers
- Those in manual jobs with high physical strain

### Symptoms
- Pain over lateral epicondyle or just below
- Pain may spread up and down the arm
- Impaired handgrip restricting daily activities

### Clinical Examination
- Tenderness over lateral epicondyle (sometimes nearby)
- **Key sign**: increased pain when attempting to dorsiflex wrist against resistance with elbow extended
- Full range of movement usually preserved
- Some severe/chronic cases may lose a few degrees of extension

### Investigations
- Not usually helpful unless excluding alternative diagnosis
- MRI can identify tissue abnormalities

### Treatment Options

**Early/Mild Cases:**
- Rest/activity modification (full rest is contraindicated)
- Splinting - counterforce brace encircling forearm below elbow to reduce overload of wrist extensors

**Success Rate:**
- Non-surgical treatment successful in 90-95% of cases
- Best results in patients with symptoms < 3 months

### Recurrence
- Mechanics, builders, and domestic workers most susceptible
- Job modifications may help reduce recurrence
