# Lateral Epicondylopathy (Tennis Elbow): A Review of Current Concepts and Treatment

## Source
JSciMed Central - Annals of Sports Medicine Research (2017)

## Key Findings

### What is Tennis Elbow?
- Lateral epicondylopathy (tennis elbow) is a common cause of lateral elbow pain in the general population and athletes
- Results from irritation of the ECRB (extensor carpi radialis brevis) tendon
- Involves angiofibroblastic changes in the forearm

### Symptoms
- Lateral elbow pain
- Pain with resisted wrist and long finger extension
- Pain may radiate up or down the arm
- Impaired handgrip affecting daily activities

### Diagnosis
- Clinical examination (no imaging typically needed)
- Tenderness over lateral epicondyle
- Increased pain when attempting to dorsiflex wrist against resistance with elbow extended
- Must differentiate from: cervical radiculopathy, radial tunnel syndrome, degenerative joint disease

### Treatment

**Non-Operative (90-95% success rate):**
- Rest
- Ice
- Physical therapy
- Splinting (counterforce brace)

**Operative Treatments (when conservative fails):**
- Percutaneous release
- Arthroscopic release
- Open debridement

### Risks of Surgery
- Potential injury to radial nerve
- Risk to lateral ulnar collateral ligament leading to posterolateral instability
- Debridement should not extend beyond midline of radial head

### Prognosis
- Best results with conservative management in patients with symptoms < 3 months
- Over 40 different treatment regimes described in literature
