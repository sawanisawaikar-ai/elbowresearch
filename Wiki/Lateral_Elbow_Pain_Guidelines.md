# Lateral Elbow Pain and Muscle Function Impairments: Clinical Practice Guidelines

## Source
Journal of Orthopaedic & Sports Physical Therapy (JOSPT) - 2022

## Overview
Evidence-based clinical practice guidelines for diagnosis and treatment of lateral elbow pain, particularly lateral epicondylitis/tendinopathy.

## Key Recommendations

### Epidemiology
- One of most common elbow conditions
- Affects 1-3% of general population
- Peak incidence: ages 30-50
- Both recreational and occupational causes

### Risk Factors
- Repetitive forearm activities
- High-force gripping
- Awkward wrist postures
- Occupational exposures
- Previous elbow injuries
- Smoking

### Diagnosis

**Clinical Tests:**
1. **Cozen's Test**: Resisted wrist extension with elbow flexed → pain at lateral epicondyle
2. **Mill's Test**: Passive stretch of wrist extensors with elbow extended → pain
3. **Palpation**: Tenderness over extensor tendon origin

**Differential Diagnoses:**
- Radial tunnel syndrome
- Cervical radiculopathy
- Ligamentous injury
- Articular pathology

### Treatment Interventions

**Strong Evidence (Recommended):**
- Eccentric exercise programs
- Manual therapy combined with exercise
- Counterforce bracing
- Activity modification

**Moderate Evidence:**
- Stretching programs
- Strengthening programs
- Ultrasound therapy
- Low-level laser therapy

**Weak/Limited Evidence:**
- Corticosteroid injections (short-term benefit only)
- Extracorporeal shock wave therapy
- Ionotophoresis
-针灸

### Exercise Guidelines

**Eccentric Strengthening:**
- Slow, controlled lowering movements
- 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
- 2x daily
- Progress as tolerated

**Isometric Exercises (Early):**
- Wrist flexion, extension
- Radial/ulnar deviation
- Hold 5-10 seconds
- Multiple directions

**Progressive Strengthening:**
- Weeks 3-4: Resistance bands
- Weeks 5-6: Weights, functional training

### Prognosis
- 90-95% success with conservative treatment
- Best results: < 3 months symptom duration
- 20-30% recurrence rate
- Long-term outcomes generally favorable

### Return to Activity
- Gradual progression
- Monitor for symptom recurrence
- Address ergonomic factors
- Preventive exercises recommended
