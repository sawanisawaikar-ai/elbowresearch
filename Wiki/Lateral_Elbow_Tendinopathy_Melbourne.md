# Management of Lateral Elbow Tendinopathy (LET)

## Source
Melbourne Hand Therapy (2020)

## Overview
Lateral elbow tendinopathy (LET) is a painful condition affecting the outer elbow, commonly seen in both athletic and occupational settings.

## Key Concepts

### What is Tendinopathy?
- Degenerative condition of the tendon
- NOT primarily inflammatory
- Results from overuse and microtrauma
- Affects the common extensor tendon origin

### Anatomy
- Primary site: Extensor Carpi Radialis Brevis (ECRB)
- Location: Lateral epicondyle of humerus
- Secondary involvement: ECRL, extensor digitorum communis

### Risk Factors

| Category | Factors |
|----------|---------|
| Occupational | Repetitive motions, forceful gripping, vibration |
| Sports | Tennis, golf, rowing |
| Demographic | Age 30-50 years |

### Clinical Features
- Lateral elbow pain (may radiate distally)
- Pain with gripping
- Pain with wrist extension against resistance
- Morning stiffness
- Point tenderness over lateral epicondyle

### Assessment Tools
- Visual Analog Scale (VAS)
- Grip strength (JAMAR)
- Algometer for pressure pain threshold
- Patient-rated tennis elbow evaluation

### Treatment Approaches

**Phase 1: Acute (Weeks 1-2)**
- Isometric exercises
- Pain management
- Activity modification
- Ice/heat therapy

**Phase 2: Subacute (Weeks 3-4)**
- Progressive stretching
- Light resistance exercises
- Wrist splinting (15-20° dorsiflexion)

**Phase 3: Chronic/Strengthening (Weeks 4-6+)**
- Eccentric strengthening
- Combined movements
- Resistance training
- Functional exercises

### Splinting
- Counterforce braces effective
- Wrist splinting showed improvement in 2 weeks
- Continued benefits at 6 weeks

### Prognosis
- 90-95% respond to conservative treatment
- Best outcomes with early intervention
- Recurrence common without activity modification
